Importance of Sleep

Sleep and Academic Performance

Adequate sleep is crucial for memory consolidation and cognitive function.

How-to:

  1. Aim for 7–9 hours of sleep each night.
  2. Establish a consistent sleep schedule.
  3. Create a restful sleep environment.

Sleep Hygiene Tips

Practices that promote consistent, uninterrupted sleep.

How-to:

  1. Limit screen time before bed.
  2. Avoid caffeine and heavy meals in the evening.
  3. Establish a bedtime routine to signal your body it's time to sleep.

Impact of Sleep Deprivation

Lack of sleep can lead to decreased concentration, mood swings, and health issues.

How-to:

  1. Recognize signs of sleep deprivation (irritability, fatigue).
  2. Prioritize sleep to maintain overall well-being.

Healthy Snacks for Focus

Nuts & Seeds

Packed with omega-3s and antioxidants, they boost brain function.

How-to:

  1. Eat a small handful of almonds, walnuts, or pumpkin seeds during study breaks.
  2. Pair with fruit for added energy.

Dark Chocolate

Contains caffeine and flavonoids that improve memory and focus.

How-to:

  1. Snack on 1–2 small squares of dark chocolate (70% cocoa or higher).
  2. Avoid overindulgence to prevent sugar crashes.

Fruit + Protein Combos

Balances quick energy (fruits) with long-lasting fuel (protein).

How-to:

  1. Pair apple slices with peanut butter.
  2. Eat Greek yogurt topped with berries.
  3. Try a banana with almond butter before studying.
