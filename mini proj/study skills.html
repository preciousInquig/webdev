Methods to Study Easier

Practical approaches and step-by-step study methods to improve comprehension and retention.

Note-taking Methods

  • Cornell Note-Taking Method

    A structured system that divides your page into three sections: cues, notes, and summary. Enhances comprehension and retention by encouraging active engagement during and after lectures.

    How-to:

    1. Divide your page into cues, notes, and summary sections.
    2. Take notes during lectures in the notes section.
    3. Add cues/questions in the left column after class.
    4. Summarize the main points at the bottom.
  • Mind Mapping

    Visually organizes information into branches and connections, helping you see relationships between topics.

    How-to:

    1. Write the main topic in the center.
    2. Draw branches for subtopics.
    3. Add keywords, images, or colors to each branch.
    4. Expand as needed to show relationships.
  • Charting Method

    Organizes information into columns and rows, making it easier to compare facts, events, or ideas.

    How-to:

    1. Draw a table with labeled columns.
    2. Fill in details row by row.
    3. Use it for subjects with lots of facts or data (e.g., history, science).

Memory Techniques

  • Spaced Repetition

    Involves reviewing information at increasing intervals to enhance long-term retention.

    How-to:

    1. Use flashcards or apps like Anki.
    2. Review material after 1 day, 3 days, 7 days, etc.
    3. Focus more on difficult items.
  • Memory Palace (Method of Loci)

    Associates information with locations in a familiar place to aid recall.

    How-to:

    1. Choose a familiar location (e.g., your home).
    2. Assign information to specific spots.
    3. Mentally walk through the place to recall.
  • Active Recall

    Actively tests memory instead of passively reviewing notes.

    How-to:

    1. After studying, close your notes and recall key points.
    2. Use flashcards or practice quizzes.
    3. Review again to reinforce learning.

Exam Prep Strategies

  • Start Early and Plan Strategically

    Begin studying well in advance with a structured plan.

    How-to:

    1. Break down the syllabus into sections.
    2. Allocate specific times for each topic.
    3. Adjust the plan as needed.
  • Organize Your Study Space

    A clutter-free environment enhances focus and productivity.

    How-to:

    1. Keep only necessary materials nearby.
    2. Ensure good lighting and ventilation.
    3. Minimize distractions like phone notifications.
